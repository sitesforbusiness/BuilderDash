# BuilderDash vs Generic CRM Software for Builders

Generic CRM tools like HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive, or Monday.com are powerful — but they're not built for construction. Using them to run a trades or contracting business means forcing a sales pipeline tool to do a job management job.

---

## What CRM Tools Are Built For

CRM software is designed for sales teams managing leads, deals, and customer relationships — typically in B2B software sales, services firms, or retail.

The core model is:
- Lead → Opportunity → Deal → Customer

That's not how construction works.

---

## How Construction Actually Works

- Client enquiry → Site visit → Quote → Variations → Job start → Phases of work → Snagging → Invoice milestones → Final invoice → Retention

You need to manage teams on site, upload photos as evidence, track materials, schedule different trades at different stages, and raise multiple invoices at different points in the same job.

No generic CRM handles this well out of the box.

---

## The Problem With Generic Tools

### You Have to Build Everything Yourself
Generic CRMs give you fields, stages, and pipelines — but you have to configure them for construction. Hours of setup to create a system that still doesn't quite fit.

### No Construction-Specific Features
- No quote-to-invoice flow built around construction billing
- No site photo management tied to jobs
- No scheduling built around trades and site teams
- No VAT handling specific to construction (e.g. domestic vs commercial rates)

### Overhead That Slows You Down
CRM tools are optimised for salespeople sitting at desks updating records after calls. They're not built for a site manager updating a job from their phone between tasks.

### Cost vs Value
Many CRM platforms charge per user per month, plus add-ons. You end up paying for features you'll never use, while the features you actually need aren't there.

## When a CRM Makes Sense

If your main need is managing a large volume of inbound leads and nurturing them over a long sales cycle, a CRM might be the right tool alongside a system like BuilderDash.

For actually running construction jobs — from quote through to paid invoice — a generic CRM is the wrong tool.
