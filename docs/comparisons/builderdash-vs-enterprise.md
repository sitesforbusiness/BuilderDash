# BuilderDash vs Enterprise Construction Software

Enterprise construction software — platforms like Procore, Aconex, Viewpoint, or Oracle Primavera — are built for large construction firms managing complex, multi-million-pound projects with large teams, formal procurement processes, and dedicated project management staff.

If you're a builder, fit-out contractor, or trade running a small to mid-size business, these tools are almost certainly the wrong fit.

---

## What Enterprise Software Is Built For

Enterprise platforms are designed for:

- Large general contractors and developers
- Projects worth millions
- Teams with dedicated project managers, quantity surveyors, contract administrators
- Complex subcontractor supply chains
- Formal document control and contract management processes

They're powerful. They're also expensive, slow to implement, and overwhelming to use without training.

---

## The Problems With Enterprise Tools for Smaller Operators

### Implementation Time
Enterprise platforms can take weeks or months to configure and implement. Most require professional services to set up. That's not weeks of your time you have to spare.

### Training Overhead
Your team needs training. Then more training. Then you discover half of them aren't using it because it's too complicated.

### Price
Licensing costs for enterprise construction software can run into thousands of pounds per year — before professional services, add-ons, and per-user fees. The ROI math only works at scale.

### Features You'll Never Use
Enterprise tools come with contract management, BIM integrations, earned value analysis, and procurement workflows. If you're running fit-outs and residential projects, you don't need any of that — but you're paying for it.

### Built for Office Teams, Not Site Teams
The people who use enterprise software day-to-day are project managers and administrators in offices. Field teams get a watered-down mobile app as an afterthought.

---

## What Smaller Builders Actually Need

A builder or fit-out contractor running 5-20 jobs a year needs:

- Fast quote creation
- Simple job tracking
- Photo documentation from site
- Straightforward invoicing
- Team scheduling without complexity

Not: BIM integration, earned value tracking, formal RFI workflows, or enterprise procurement management.
