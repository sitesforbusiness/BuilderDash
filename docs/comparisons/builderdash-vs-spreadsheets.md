# BuilderDash vs Spreadsheets for Construction Management

Most small construction businesses start with spreadsheets. They're free, flexible, and everyone knows how to use them. But as your business grows, spreadsheets start costing you more than they save.

---

## The Spreadsheet Setup

The typical spreadsheet setup for a builder or contractor:

- One spreadsheet for quotes (manually typed, manually updated)
- A separate one for jobs and their status
- Another for invoices (or just Word/PDF templates)
- A shared folder for photos (or just WhatsApp)
- Scheduling done via text message or a whiteboard

It works. Until it doesn't.

---

## Where Spreadsheets Break Down

### Version Control
Which version of the quote is the one the client approved? Did you update the job spreadsheet when the quote changed? Is the invoice using the right figures?

With spreadsheets, these are real questions with no reliable answers.

### No Connection Between Documents
Your quote, job record, and invoice are three separate files with no link between them. Change something in one and you have to manually update the others — or you forget to.

### Photos Are Everywhere
Site photos live on phones, in WhatsApp, maybe in a Dropbox folder — if someone remembered to upload them. Finding a specific photo from a job three months ago is a mission.

### No Live Overview
You can't see at a glance which jobs are active, which are overdue, which haven't been invoiced yet. You have to open files and piece it together.

### Doesn't Scale
When you're running one or two jobs, spreadsheets are manageable. When you're running five, six, seven jobs simultaneously — things fall through the cracks.

## The Real Cost of Spreadsheets

Spreadsheets look free. But the real cost is:

- **Time** spent re-entering the same information in multiple places
- **Errors** from manual re-entry (quoting the wrong figure, invoicing the wrong amount)
- **Lost revenue** from invoices that aren't chased because you forgot they were outstanding
- **Disputes** you can't defend because you don't have a clear record of what was agreed or what was done

---

## When to Make the Switch

If any of these are true, you've outgrown spreadsheets:

- You're running more than 2-3 jobs at the same time
- You've ever sent an invoice with the wrong figures
- You've had to dig through WhatsApp to find a site photo
- You've forgotten to invoice a completed job
- A client has disputed something you couldn't prove
