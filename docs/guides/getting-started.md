# Getting Started with BuilderDash

Welcome to BuilderDash — construction project management software built for builders, tradespeople, and fit-out contractors.

This guide walks you through setting up your account and running your first job from quote to invoice.

---

## Step 1: Create Your Account

Go to [builderdash.app](https://builderdash.app) and sign up. You'll need:

- Your business name
- Your email address
- A password

No credit card required to get started.

---

## Step 2: Set Up Your Business Profile

Once you're in, fill in your business details:

- **Business name** — appears on all quotes and invoices
- **Address** — included on documents for professional appearance
- **Logo** — upload your logo to brand your documents
- **Payment details** — add bank details so clients know how to pay

Getting this right upfront means every quote and invoice you send looks professional from day one.

---

## Step 3: Add Your First Client

Before you can quote or raise a job, you need a client on the system.

Go to **Clients** → **Add Client** and enter:

- Client name (person or company)
- Contact details (email, phone)
- Address (for the job site or billing)

---

## Step 4: Create Your First Quote

Go to **Quotes** → **New Quote**.

1. Select the client
2. Add a job description
3. Add line items (materials, labour, etc.) with quantities and prices
4. Set your payment terms
5. Preview and send

The client receives a professional quote they can approve. When they do, you convert it to a job in one click.

See: [Creating a Quote →](creating-a-quote.md)

---

## Step 5: Manage the Job

Once a quote is accepted, it becomes a job. From the job view you can:

- Track progress and status
- Assign team members
- Upload site photos and documents
- Log client communication
- Add notes

See: [Managing Jobs →](managing-jobs.md)

---

## Step 6: Invoice the Client

When the work is done (or at agreed milestones), raise an invoice directly from the job. All the details carry over — no re-entry needed.

See: [Invoicing Clients →](invoicing.md)

---

## You're Running

That's the core loop: **Quote → Job → Invoice**.

Everything else in BuilderDash supports that flow — scheduling, photos, documents, client communication — but that's the engine.
