# Site Photos & Documents in BuilderDash

Every photo and document in BuilderDash is tied to a specific job — no more hunting through camera rolls, WhatsApp threads, or email chains to find what you need.

---

## Why This Matters

On a construction job, documentation protects you:

- **Before photos** — prove the condition of the site before you started
- **Progress photos** — show the client work is on track
- **Completion photos** — record the finished state
- **Issue photos** — document problems you found (hidden damage, client-caused issues, etc.)
- **Signed documents** — contracts, variation orders, client sign-offs

Keeping all of this inside the job means it's always where you need it.

---

## Uploading Site Photos

### From the Office (Desktop)
Open the job → go to **Photos** → drag and drop or select files to upload.

### From Site (Mobile)
Open the job on your phone → tap **Add Photo** → take a photo or select from your camera roll.

Photos upload directly to the job. No emailing to yourself. No WhatsApp.

---

## Organising Photos

You can add a caption to each photo so it's clear what it shows — especially useful when you're uploading multiple progress shots across a long job.

Photos are shown in the order they were uploaded with timestamps, so you have a clear timeline of the job.

---

## Documents

Attach any document to a job:

- **Contracts** — the signed agreement with the client
- **Specifications** — architect or designer drawings and specs
- **Variation orders** — signed approvals for work outside the original quote
- **Delivery notes** — for materials received on site
- **Subcontractor quotes** — if you're managing subbies on the job
- **Health & safety documents** — risk assessments, method statements

Accepted formats: PDF, Word, Excel, images, and most common file types.

---

## Sharing with Clients

You can share specific photos or documents with the client directly from the job — useful for progress updates or sending completion photos before raising the final invoice.

---

## Protecting Yourself

The most valuable use of site documentation is dispute resolution. If a client claims work wasn't done, or wasn't done correctly, your photo record — timestamped and tied to the job — is your evidence.

Document:
- The site **before** you started (especially if there's existing damage)
- Any issues or hidden problems discovered during the job
- Completed work at each stage
- Client sign-offs

---

## Storage

All photos and documents are stored securely in BuilderDash. You're not relying on someone's phone not getting lost or broken.
