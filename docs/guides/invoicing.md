# Invoicing Clients in BuilderDash

BuilderDash generates invoices directly from jobs — no re-entry, no separate system, no copy-pasting figures into a template.

---

## Raising an Invoice

From any completed job (or at a payment milestone), go to the job and click **Raise Invoice**.

BuilderDash pulls through:
- Client details
- Job description
- Line items from the original quote
- Your business details and branding

Review it, adjust if needed (e.g. variations that weren't in the original quote), and send.

---

## Invoice Types

### Full Invoice
Covers the entire job value. Use when billing at completion.

### Deposit Invoice
Raise a partial invoice upfront before work starts. Useful for covering material costs or securing commitment on larger jobs.

### Stage / Milestone Invoice
Raise invoices at agreed points during the job — common on larger fit-outs or multi-week projects. For example:
- 30% on mobilisation
- 40% on first fix completion
- 30% on handover

### Variation Invoice
For work done outside the original quote scope. Raise a separate invoice for the additional work.

---

## Invoice Details

Each invoice includes:

- **Your business name and address**
- **Invoice number** — auto-generated, sequential
- **Invoice date and due date**
- **Client name and address**
- **Line items with quantities and prices**
- **Subtotal, VAT, and total**
- **Payment details** — bank transfer details so clients know how to pay
- **Your logo** (if uploaded to your profile)

---

## Sending Invoices

**Option 1 — Send from BuilderDash**
Email the invoice directly to your client from within the system. It arrives as a professional PDF attachment.

**Option 2 — Download as PDF**
Download the invoice and send it yourself via your usual email — useful if you want to add a personal message or send from your own email client.

---

## Tracking Payment Status

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Draft | Not yet sent |
| Sent | Sent to client |
| Overdue | Past the due date, unpaid |
| Paid | Payment marked as received |

Mark invoices as paid when you receive payment. BuilderDash updates the job status automatically.

---

## Chasing Overdue Invoices

When an invoice goes overdue, BuilderDash flags it in your dashboard. You can see at a glance which clients owe you money and how long it's been outstanding.

---

## VAT

BuilderDash supports VAT. Set the applicable VAT rate on each line item. The invoice calculates and displays:
- Net amount
- VAT amount
- Gross total

If you're VAT registered, your VAT number can be added to your business profile and appears on every invoice.

---

## Tips

- **Raise deposit invoices before starting** — don't fund the client's project out of your own pocket.
- **Set clear payment terms on the quote** — so there's no argument when the invoice arrives.
- **Invoice promptly** — the longer you wait after completion, the longer you wait to get paid.
- **Mark jobs as paid in BuilderDash** — keeps your pipeline view accurate.
