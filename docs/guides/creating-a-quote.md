# Creating a Quote in BuilderDash

Quoting is where every job starts. BuilderDash makes it fast to produce a professional quote and get it in front of your client.

---

## Before You Start

Make sure you have:
- The client added to your account (see [Getting Started](getting-started.md))
- A rough breakdown of what the job involves (labour, materials, subcontractors)

---

## Creating the Quote

Go to **Quotes** → **New Quote**.

### 1. Select the Client
Pick an existing client from your list, or add a new one on the spot.

### 2. Add Job Details
- **Job title** — what the work is (e.g. "Kitchen Fit-Out — 14 Maple Road")
- **Description** — overview of the scope of work
- **Job address** — if different from the client's billing address

### 3. Add Line Items
This is the breakdown of costs. Add each item with:

- **Description** — what it is (e.g. "Labour — first fix electrical", "Materials — timber framing")
- **Quantity**
- **Unit price**
- **VAT rate** (if applicable)

BuilderDash calculates totals automatically. Add as many line items as you need.

### 4. Set Payment Terms
Choose from common terms or set custom ones:
- Due on receipt
- 14 days
- 30 days
- Stage payments / deposit structure

### 5. Add Notes or Exclusions
Use the notes field to clarify what's included and excluded. This protects you if there's a dispute later.

---

## Sending the Quote

When you're happy with it:

1. **Preview** — check how it looks as a document
2. **Send** — email it directly to the client from BuilderDash, or download as PDF to send yourself

The client receives a professional quote document with your branding.

---

## Tracking Quote Status

In the **Quotes** list you can see the status of every quote:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Draft | Not yet sent |
| Sent | Sent to client, awaiting response |
| Accepted | Client approved — ready to convert to job |
| Declined | Client turned it down |
| Expired | Past the validity date |

---

## Converting to a Job

When a quote is accepted, click **Convert to Job**. All the details — client, description, line items — carry over automatically. No re-entry.

See: [Managing Jobs →](managing-jobs.md)

---

## Tips

- **Be specific on line items** — vague quotes lead to disputes. Break it down properly.
- **Always include exclusions** — state what's NOT included (e.g. "structural work excluded", "customer to supply tiles").
- **Set a validity date** — quotes with no expiry can come back to bite you when material costs have changed.
