# Scheduling Your Team in BuilderDash

BuilderDash scheduling keeps your team organised across multiple jobs without needing a separate tool, group chats, or a whiteboard.

---

## How Scheduling Works

Scheduling in BuilderDash connects your **jobs** to your **team members** with **tasks** and **dates**.

- Each job has tasks
- Each task is assigned to a person
- Each task has a date (or date range)
- Your schedule view shows who's doing what and when

---

## Setting Up Your Team

Before you can schedule, add your team members under **Settings** → **Team**.

For each person add:
- Name
- Role / trade (e.g. electrician, labourer, site manager)
- Contact details

---

## Assigning Work

### From a Job
Open the job → go to **Tasks** → add a task → assign it to a team member and set the date.

The assigned person appears in the schedule for that date against that job.

### From the Schedule
Open **Schedule** → pick a date → assign a team member to a job. This creates a task automatically tied to the job.

---

## The Schedule View

The schedule shows you a calendar view across your team:

- **Who is assigned to which job on which day**
- **Any unassigned tasks** that need a person
- **Gaps** — days with no work assigned (useful for planning new jobs)
- **Conflicts** — if someone is double-booked

Switch between daily, weekly, and monthly views depending on what you need to see.

---

## Managing Changes

Construction jobs never go exactly to plan. When a job runs over, gets delayed, or a team member is off:

1. Open the affected tasks
2. Reassign or reschedule
3. The schedule updates immediately

Everyone sees the change next time they look at their schedule.

---

## Subcontractors

You can add subcontractors to your team list and schedule them the same way as your own staff. Useful for keeping track of when subbies are on which site.

---

## Mobile Access

Your team can check their schedule on mobile. They see:
- What job they're on
- Where the site is
- What tasks they're expected to complete
- Any notes you've added

No phone calls needed to confirm where people should be in the morning.

---

## Tips

- **Schedule a week ahead minimum** — gives your team visibility and reduces last-minute calls
- **Add site addresses to jobs** — so your team can navigate directly from the schedule
- **Use task notes** — add detail about what specifically needs doing, not just which job
- **Check for gaps** — use quiet periods to pull forward prep work on upcoming jobs
