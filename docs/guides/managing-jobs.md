# Managing Jobs in BuilderDash

A job in BuilderDash is the central record for a piece of work. Everything related to that project — team, photos, documents, communication, invoices — lives in one place.

---

## Job Lifecycle

```
Quote Accepted → Job Created → In Progress → Complete → Invoiced → Paid
```

BuilderDash tracks the job through each stage so you always know where things stand across your whole workload.

---

## The Job View

When you open a job, you see:

- **Job details** — client, address, description, dates
- **Status** — current stage of the job
- **Assigned team** — who's on this job
- **Tasks** — what needs doing and when
- **Site photos** — photos uploaded from site
- **Documents** — contracts, specs, anything relevant
- **Communication log** — notes and messages with the client
- **Financials** — linked quote and invoices

---

## Job Statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Pending | Job created, not yet started |
| In Progress | Active — work underway |
| On Hold | Paused for any reason |
| Complete | Work finished |
| Invoiced | Invoice raised |
| Paid | Payment received |

Update the status as the job moves forward. Gives you a real-time view of your whole pipeline.

---

## Assigning Team Members

From the job view, go to **Team** and assign which of your people are working on this job. They'll see it in their schedule.

---

## Tasks

Break the job down into tasks — individual actions that need completing.

Each task has:
- Description
- Assigned person
- Due date
- Status (to do / in progress / done)

Use tasks to manage what's happening day to day on a job without losing the big picture.

---

## Site Photos

Upload photos directly to the job from the site. Works on mobile — your team can upload on the go.

Good for:
- Progress documentation
- Before/after records
- Evidence if there's a dispute
- Showing clients how the work is progressing

See: [Site Photos & Documents →](site-photos.md)

---

## Client Communication

Log every interaction with the client directly on the job. Notes, calls, emails — keep a record so nothing gets lost and there's always context when you need it.

---

## Raising an Invoice

When the job is complete (or at a payment milestone), go to **Invoice** and raise one directly from the job. Line items, client details, and job information carry over — no re-entry.

See: [Invoicing Clients →](invoicing.md)

---

## Multiple Jobs at Once

The **Jobs Dashboard** gives you a live view of all active jobs — statuses, what's overdue, what's coming up. When you're running five jobs simultaneously, this is how you stay on top of it without things falling through the cracks.
