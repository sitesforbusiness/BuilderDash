# Job Management — BuilderDash

The job is the core unit in BuilderDash. Every piece of work you do — from a small repair to a full fit-out — is managed as a job. Everything related to that work lives in one place.

---

## What a Job Contains

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| Job details | Client, address, description, dates |
| Status | Where the job is in its lifecycle |
| Linked quote | The original quote this job came from |
| Team | Who is assigned to this job |
| Tasks | Specific work items with assignees and dates |
| Site photos | Progress and documentation photos |
| Documents | Contracts, specs, delivery notes, etc. |
| Communication log | Notes and messages with the client |
| Invoices | All invoices raised against this job |

---

## Job Lifecycle

```
Created → In Progress → Complete → Invoiced → Paid
```

Jobs move through statuses as work progresses. Your dashboard shows the live status of every job, so you always know where things stand across your whole business.

---

## Job Dashboard

The job dashboard gives you a real-time view of your workload:

- **Active jobs** — what's currently in progress
- **Upcoming** — jobs scheduled to start soon
- **Awaiting invoice** — completed jobs that haven't been invoiced yet
- **Overdue** — jobs past their expected completion date

This replaces the mental overhead of keeping track of everything. The system does it.

---

## Tasks

Break each job down into tasks — the specific things that need to happen for the job to be complete.

Each task has:
- **Description** of the work
- **Assigned team member**
- **Due date**
- **Status** — To Do, In Progress, Done

Tasks give your team clarity on what's expected and when, and give you visibility of progress without being on site.

---

## Multi-Phase Jobs

For larger jobs that run over weeks or months, use tasks and status updates to track phases. For example:

**Office Fit-Out — Phases:**
- Strip out
- First fix mechanical and electrical
- Partitioning
- Second fix M&E
- Finishes
- Snagging

Each phase can be a group of tasks with its own assignees and dates.

---

## Running Multiple Jobs

BuilderDash is built for contractors running several jobs simultaneously. The dashboard and schedule views let you manage multiple jobs without losing track of any of them.

Common for:
- Residential builders with multiple sites
- Fit-out contractors running parallel projects
- Trade contractors (electricians, plumbers) with a high volume of smaller jobs

---

## Job Profitability

Track what a job should have cost (from the quote) against what you're seeing on the ground. Useful for understanding which types of jobs are most profitable and where costs tend to run over.

---

## Archiving Completed Jobs

Completed and paid jobs are archived but never deleted. You can always go back and look at what was done, what was charged, and what documentation exists for any past job.
