# Team Scheduling — BuilderDash

BuilderDash scheduling connects your jobs to your team so you always know who's on what site and when — without a whiteboard, a group chat, or a phone call.

---

## Overview

Scheduling in BuilderDash is built around your jobs, not a separate calendar system. When you schedule work, you're assigning tasks from real jobs to real people on real dates.

Everything stays connected.

---

## Setting Up

Before scheduling, add your team:

**Settings → Team → Add Member**

For each team member:
- Name
- Role / trade
- Contact details

You can also add subcontractors to your team list and schedule them the same way.

---

## The Schedule View

The schedule shows you a calendar view of your entire team across all jobs:

- **Who is on which job on each day**
- **Unallocated tasks** that need someone assigned
- **Gaps** in the schedule (days with no work assigned)
- **Conflicts** if someone is double-booked

Switch between daily, weekly, and monthly views.

---

## Assigning Work

### Method 1: From the Job
Open the job → Tasks → create a task → assign to a team member → set the date.

The task appears in the schedule for that person on that date.

### Method 2: From the Schedule
Open the schedule → pick a date → assign a team member to a job.

Both methods produce the same result. Use whichever fits your workflow.

---

## Tasks and the Schedule

Tasks are the unit of scheduling. Each task:

- Belongs to a specific job
- Is assigned to a specific person
- Has a date (or date range)
- Has a status

The schedule is a visual representation of all tasks across all jobs and all team members.

---

## Managing Changes

When a job runs over, is delayed, or a team member is unavailable:

1. Open the affected tasks
2. Reassign or change the date
3. Schedule updates immediately
4. Team sees the change on their schedule

---

## Team View (Mobile)

Your team members can check their schedule on mobile. They see:

- Which job they're on today
- Where the site is (with address for navigation)
- What tasks they're expected to complete
- Any notes you've added

Reduces the morning phone calls about where people should be.

---

## Multi-Trade Scheduling

If you work with multiple trades on the same job (electricians, plumbers, carpenters), you can schedule all of them against the same job at different times — and see at a glance whether sequencing makes sense.

For example:
- First fix plumbing: Week 1
- First fix electrical: Week 1-2
- Boarding: Week 2
- Second fix plumbing: Week 4
- Second fix electrical: Week 4

---

## Scheduling and Invoicing

When tasks are marked complete, it informs the job status — which tells you when a job is ready to invoice. The scheduling and invoicing systems stay connected rather than being separate processes.

---

## Capacity Planning

Use the monthly view to see how loaded your team is and where you have capacity to take on new work — before you commit to a new job or start date.
